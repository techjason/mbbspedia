## Management of Ischaemic Heart Disease

### Guiding Principles

The management of IHD rests on **two pillars** [2]:
1. **Improve quality of life** ‚Üí symptomatic relief (anti-anginal therapy)
2. **Improve life expectancy** ‚Üí prognostic therapy (prevent MI, death, heart failure)

The approach differs fundamentally between **stable IHD (chronic coronary syndrome)** and **acute coronary syndrome (ACS = UA/NSTEMI/STEMI)**. In stable disease, you have time to optimise medical therapy and decide on revascularisation electively. In ACS, the immediate priorities are to **restore coronary flow, limit infarct size, prevent death, and treat complications** [1][2][9].

#### A. General Measures [2]

| Measure | Detail | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| ***Lifestyle: stop smoking, regular exercise (but not beyond point of discomfort)*** [2] | Smoking cessation drastically ‚ÜďMI risk after just 1 year [2]. Moderate exercise 30 min √ó 5/week | Smoking causes endothelial injury, ‚ÜĎthrombosis, ‚ÜĎvasospasm. Exercise ‚ÜĎHDL, ‚Üďinsulin resistance, improves endothelial function |
| ***Treat precipitating factors: thyrotoxicosis, anaemia*** [2] | Check TFT and CBC in all patients with angina [2] | Thyrotoxicosis ‚ÜĎO‚āā demand (‚ÜĎHR, ‚ÜĎcontractility). Anaemia ‚ÜďO‚āā supply. Correcting these may abolish angina entirely without need for further intervention |
| ***DM: aim A1c < 7%, consider SGLT2i or GLP-1a*** [2] | SGLT2i (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) and GLP-1 RA (semaglutide, liraglutide) have proven cardiovascular outcome benefits | DM accelerates atherosclerosis via multiple mechanisms. SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA independently reduce MACE (major adverse cardiovascular events) |
| ***HTN: aim < 140/90, use BB if indicated*** [2] | ő≤-blocker preferred if coexistent angina; otherwise ACEI/ARB | HTN ‚ÜĎafterload ‚Üí ‚ÜĎwall stress ‚Üí ‚ÜĎO‚āā demand. Also accelerates endothelial dysfunction |
| ***Lipids: ‚ÜďLDL to < 1.8 mmol/L with lifestyle and drug*** [2] | ESC 2019/2021: very high risk = LDL < 1.4 mmol/L AND ‚Č• 50% reduction [17] | LDL is the primary driver of atherosclerosis; aggressive lowering stabilises plaques and reduces events |

#### B. Pharmacological Therapy ‚ÄĒ Prognostic Treatment [2]

These drugs **reduce the risk of MI and death** and should be given to **all** patients with established CAD unless contraindicated.

##### 1. Antiplatelet Therapy [2]

| Drug | Mechanism | Regimen | Key Points |
|---|---|---|---|
| ***Aspirin*** | Irreversible COX-1 inhibition ‚Üí ‚Üďthromboxane A‚āā (TXA‚āā) synthesis ‚Üí ‚Üďplatelet aggregation | ***Low-dose (75‚Äď325 mg daily) prescribed for all patients with CAD indefinitely*** [2] | First-line antiplatelet. S/E: GI bleeding, peptic ulcer ‚Üí consider PPI cover |
| ***Clopidogrel (Plavix)*** | Irreversible P2Y‚āĀ‚āā ADP receptor antagonist on platelets ‚Üí ‚ÜďADP-mediated platelet activation | 75 mg daily | ***Equally/more effective; used as alternative to aspirin if intolerant (‚ÜĎcost)*** [2]. ***Combination therapy (DAPT): standard of care post-ACS/PCI, but not associated with benefit in stable CAD*** [2] |

> **Why is DAPT not used in stable CAD alone?** In stable disease, the plaque surface is intact with no acute thrombus. Single antiplatelet (aspirin) provides adequate protection. Adding a second antiplatelet increases bleeding risk without proportionate benefit. The exception is post-PCI, where the denuded stent surface is thrombogenic.

##### 2. Statins [2][17]

***Statins: recommended in all patients*** [2]

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| **MoA** | ***Inhibit HMG-CoA reductase ‚Üí ‚Üďintracellular cholesterol synthesis ‚Üí ‚ÜĎLDL receptor expression ‚Üí ‚ÜĎLDL-C clearance from blood. Also: plaque stabilisation, ‚Üďinflammation, reversal of endothelial dysfunction, ‚Üďthrombogenicity*** [17] ‚ÄĒ "pleiotropic effects" |
| ***Target*** | ***LDL < 1.8 mmol/L and/or > 50% reduction if goal cannot be achieved*** [2]. Updated ESC 2019: < 1.4 mmol/L for very high-risk patients |
| **Choice** | High-intensity: ***atorvastatin 40‚Äď80 mg*** or ***rosuvastatin 20‚Äď40 mg*** [17]. Generally more conservative dosing in Asians (higher plasma concentrations at same dose) [17] |
| **S/E** | ***Generally very well-tolerated (serious S/E < 2%)*** [17]. Myopathy (myalgia ‚Üí myositis ‚Üí rhabdomyolysis), hepatotoxicity (‚ÜĎALT), ‚ÜĎdiabetes risk |
| **Monitoring** | ***Baseline LFT and CK before starting*** [2]. Repeat LFT at 3 months, then periodically |

***If LDL target not achieved on maximum tolerated statin*** [17]:
- **Add ezetimibe** (blocks intestinal cholesterol absorption via NPC1L1 transporter) ‚ÄĒ typically ‚ÜďLDL by further 15‚Äď20%
- **Add PCSK9 inhibitor** (evolocumab, alirocumab ‚ÄĒ monoclonal antibodies that ‚ÜĎLDL receptor recycling ‚Üí ‚ÜĎLDL clearance) ‚ÄĒ can ‚ÜďLDL by 50‚Äď60% on top of statin. Reserved for very high-risk patients not at target on statin + ezetimibe

##### 3. ACEI/ARB [2]

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| **MoA** | ACEI: inhibits ACE ‚Üí ‚Üďangiotensin II ‚Üí ‚Üďvasoconstriction, ‚Üďaldosterone ‚Üí ‚Üďpreload/afterload, ‚Üďcardiac remodelling, ‚Üďendothelial dysfunction. ARB: blocks AT‚āĀ receptor directly |
| ***Indication*** | ***Evidence unclear in stable CAD alone without comorbidities*** [2]. ***Indications: when comorbidities are present, e.g. DM, HTN, LV HF where otherwise indicated*** [2] |
| **Regimen** | Ramipril, perindopril, or enalapril (ACEI); valsartan, losartan (ARB). Use one, not both ‚ÄĒ ***combination associated with ‚ÜĎadverse events without ‚ÜĎbenefits*** [2] |
| **C/I** | Bilateral renal artery stenosis, hyperkalaemia, pregnancy, angioedema (ACEI) |

#### C. Pharmacological Therapy ‚ÄĒ Anti-Anginal (Symptomatic) Treatment [1][2]

These drugs **relieve angina symptoms** but have limited or no proven effect on mortality (except ő≤-blockers post-MI).

##### 1. Nitrates [1][2]

Breaking down the mechanism from first principles:
- Nitrates release **nitric oxide (NO)** ‚Üí activates guanylate cyclase ‚Üí ‚ÜĎcGMP ‚Üí smooth muscle relaxation
- **Venodilation (major effect)** ‚Üí ‚Üďvenous return ‚Üí ‚Üďpreload ‚Üí ‚ÜďLVEDV ‚Üí ‚Üďwall stress ‚Üí ‚ÜďO‚āā demand
- **Arteriodilation (modest)** ‚Üí ‚Üďafterload ‚Üí ‚ÜďO‚āā demand
- **Coronary vasodilation** ‚Üí ‚ÜĎsupply, especially to ischaemic zones by redistributing perfusion from epicardial to endocardial sites [2]

| Form | Drug | Use | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| ***Short-acting*** | ***Sublingual nitroglycerin (GTN) 0.3‚Äď0.6 mg Q5min, max 1.2 mg within 15 min*** [2] | ***For ALL patients with symptomatic stable CAD ‚ÄĒ acute effort angina or prophylactically before exertion*** [2] | ***Should rest sitting while taking nitrates (standing ‚Üí syncope from ‚ÜďBP; supine ‚Üí ‚ÜĎVR ‚Üí ‚ÜĎpreload ‚Üí negates the effect)*** [2] |
| ***Long-acting*** | Isosorbide mononitrate (ISMN) 20‚Äď60 mg daily | ***Usually as 2nd line if BB/CCB are ineffective*** [2] | ***Must have nitrate-free or nitrate-low interval of 8‚Äď10h*** to avoid tolerance [2]. ***Risk of worsening endothelial dysfunction with long-term use*** [2] |

***S/E***: ***headache, dizziness, flushing, hypotension*** [1]

***C/I***: ***HOCM*** (‚Üďpreload ‚Üí ‚ÜďLV cavity size ‚Üí ‚ÜĎLVOT obstruction), ***PDE5 inhibitors*** (***e.g. Viagra within 24h, tadalafil within 48h*** [1]) ‚ÄĒ both cause vasodilation ‚Üí combined use ‚Üí severe life-threatening hypotension

##### 2. ő≤-Blockers [1][2]

***Beta-blocker (cardio-selective): proven survival benefit [in post-MI patients], ‚ÜďHR/BP/contractility and improve coronary perfusion*** [1]

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| **MoA** | Block ő≤‚āĀ-adrenoceptors ‚Üí ‚ÜďHR (negative chronotropy) ‚Üí ‚Üďmyocardial O‚āā demand AND ‚ÜĎdiastolic filling time ‚Üí ‚ÜĎcoronary perfusion. ‚ÜďContractility (negative inotropy) ‚Üí ‚ÜďO‚āā demand. ‚ÜďBP ‚Üí ‚Üďafterload |
| ***Role*** | ***1st line anti-anginal treatment in patients without C/I*** [2]. Clearly effective in ‚Üďexercise-induced angina, ‚ÜĎexercise tolerance, limit ischaemic episodes [2]. ***Definitely prognostic in post-MI, but effect unclear in stable CAD only*** [2] |
| ***Choice*** | ***ő≤‚āĀ-selective: metoprolol (Betaloc), bisoprolol (Zebeta)***; ***őĪ‚āĀő≤-selective: carvedilol*** [2] |
| ***Target*** | ***Titrate to HR < 70*** [1] |
| ***S/E*** | ***Precipitates ADHF, bronchospasm, exacerbate PAD, fatigue, sexual dysfunction, hypoglycaemia masking, hyperkalaemia*** [2] |

***Contraindications of beta-blockers*** [1]:
- ***Poor ventricular function*** (in acute decompensation ‚ÄĒ but NOT in stable, compensated HF where BB improve survival)
- ***Acute pulmonary oedema***
- ***Heart block (2nd / 3rd degree)***
- ***Asthma / COPD*** (severe obstructive airways disease ‚ÄĒ ő≤‚āā blockade ‚Üí bronchospasm)

<Callout title="BB in Heart Failure ‚ÄĒ A Common Confusion" type="error">
ő≤-blockers are contraindicated in **acute decompensated** heart failure (they worsen pump function acutely). However, in **stable, compensated HFrEF**, specific ő≤-blockers (bisoprolol, carvedilol, metoprolol succinate) are **life-saving** ‚ÄĒ they reverse maladaptive sympathetic activation and reduce mortality. The key is to start low and go slow, only when the patient is euvolaemic and stable. ***BB is NOT C/I in stable HF, COPD, or peripheral vascular disease*** [2].
</Callout>

##### 3. Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs) [2]

CCBs block L-type calcium channels. There are two functionally distinct classes:

| Class | Drugs | Effects | Use in IHD | C/I |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ***Dihydropyridine (DHP)*** | Amlodipine, nifedipine, felodipine | ***Mainly vascular: vasodilation with little cardiac effect*** [2]. Reflex tachycardia possible | ***Usually combined with BB*** [2] (BB counteracts reflex tachycardia). ***Safe in patients with poor cardiac function*** [2] | ***Severe AS, HOCM*** (‚ÜďPVR ‚Üí ‚Üď‚ÜďBP due to fixed ‚ÜďSV) [2] |
| ***Non-DHP*** | Diltiazem, verapamil | ***Vascular + cardiac: ‚ÜďHR, ‚Üďcontractility, ‚ÜďAVN conduction*** [2] | ***Alternative to BB in those who cannot take BB*** [2]. Anti-anginal properties similar to BB | ***C/I: HFrEF*** (negative inotropy worsens pump failure) [2]. ***NOT combined with BB ‚Üí 3rd degree HB risk*** [2] |

***Alternative: rate-limiting CCB (diltiazem / verapamil)*** used ***if BB contraindicated*** [1]

##### 4. Second-Line Anti-Anginal Agents [2]

***Usually used as 2nd line to BB/CCB combination*** [2]:

| Drug | Mechanism | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ***Ranolazine*** | ***Inhibits late inward Na‚Āļ channel ‚Üí ‚ÜďNa/Ca exchange ‚Üí ‚Üďintracellular Ca¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí ‚Üďcontractility ‚Üí ‚Üďangina*** [2] | Also ‚Üďrecurrent ischaemia, possible ‚Üďarrhythmia but ***associated with ‚ÜĎQTc*** [2] |
| ***Trimetazidine (Vastarel)*** | ***‚ÜďFatty acid oxidation ‚Üí protect myocardium from ischaemic injury*** [2] | Metabolic modulator ‚ÄĒ shifts energy substrate from FA to glucose (more O‚āā-efficient) |
| ***Nicorandil*** | ***Opens K‚Āļ channel ‚Üí arteriovenous + coronary dilatation*** [2] | Dual mechanism: K‚Āļ channel opening + nitrate-like effect |
| ***Ivabradine*** | ***Blocks HCN channel ‚Üí ‚ÜďIf ‚Üí ‚ÜďHR*** [2] | ***Used if sinus rhythm ‚Č• 70 bpm. Should be limited to HF patients as latest trials showed possible ‚ÜĎCVD death and non-fatal MI*** [2] |

#### D. Revascularisation in Stable IHD [1][2]

Revascularisation = restoring coronary blood flow, either by PCI or CABG.

***Indications for revascularisation*** [2]:

| Group | Purpose | When |
|---|---|---|
| ***High-risk anatomy*** | ***Improve prognosis*** | ***Left main stem disease (‚Č• 50% stenosis), proximal LAD disease (‚Č• 70%), 3-vessel disease (‚Č• 70%)*** [1][2] ‚ÄĒ these carry high mortality on OMT alone |
| ***Symptomatic despite OMT*** | ***Improve symptoms*** | ***Medically refractory angina*** [1] ‚ÄĒ persistent symptoms despite maximal tolerated anti-anginal therapy |
| ***Large area of ischaemia*** | ***Both*** | > 10% ischaemic myocardium on functional testing |

### PART II: MANAGEMENT OF ACUTE CORONARY SYNDROME

#### A. Initial Management of ACS (SAQ!!) [1][9]

This is extremely high-yield for exams. The initial approach applies to **all ACS** (STEMI, NSTEMI, UA):

***Admit CCU for high-risk cases*** [1]
***Complete bed rest + NPO for first 12h*** [1]
***Close monitoring: BP/P, IO Q1h, cardiac monitoring with defibrillator standby*** [1]
***Target Hx and PE to rule out other life-threatening emergencies: aortic dissection, pulmonary embolism, tension pneumothorax, perforated peptic ulcer / esophagus*** [1]

**Acute pharmacological therapy** ‚ÄĒ the mnemonic **"MONA-B"** (Morphine, Oxygen, Nitrates, Aspirin, Beta-blocker) is a useful framework:

| Agent | Detail | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Oxygen** | Supplement to keep SaO‚āā > 90% and pO‚āā > 60 mmHg [2]. Do NOT give routinely if SpO‚āā > 94% (DETO‚āāX-AMI trial) | ‚ÜĎO‚āā delivery to ischaemic myocardium. But hyperoxia ‚Üí coronary vasoconstriction ‚Üí potentially harmful |
| ***Nitrates*** | IV GTN infusion, titrated to pain relief and BP (avoid if SBP < 90) | ‚ÜďPreload (venodilation) ‚Üí ‚ÜďO‚āā demand. Some coronary vasodilation ‚Üí ‚ÜĎsupply |
| ***Aspirin*** | ***Aspirin at/suspect diagnosis*** [2]. Loading dose 300 mg PO stat, then 80‚Äď100 mg daily indefinitely | Irreversible COX-1 inhibition ‚Üí ‚ÜďTXA‚āā ‚Üí ‚Üďplatelet aggregation on the acute thrombus. Mortality benefit proven (ISIS-2 trial) |
| ***P2Y‚āĀ‚āā blocker*** | ***P2Y‚āĀ‚āā blocker at diagnosis or at PCI*** [2]. **Ticagrelor** 180 mg loading then 90 mg BD (preferred for ACS ‚ÄĒ faster onset, reversible). **Clopidogrel** 300‚Äď600 mg loading then 75 mg daily (if ticagrelor C/I or patient needs OAC). **Prasugrel** 60 mg loading then 10 mg daily (if going for PCI, no prior stroke) | Blocks ADP-mediated platelet activation via P2Y‚āĀ‚āā receptor ‚Üí more complete platelet inhibition when combined with aspirin (DAPT) |
| ***Beta-blocker*** | ***Metoprolol 25 mg BD oral, titrate to HR < 70*** [1]. Give within 24h if no C/I | ‚ÜďHR ‚Üí ‚ÜďO‚āā demand + ‚ÜĎdiastolic perfusion time. ‚ÜďRisk of arrhythmia. Mortality benefit in post-MI (COMMIT, CAPRICORN) |
| ***Heparin*** | ***LMWH (enoxaparin 1 mg/kg SC Q12h) or UFH*** [1]. ***Choice: UFH if primary PCI (faster onset), LMWH if thrombolysis, UFH/LMWH if not for reperfusion*** [1] | Anticoagulation prevents propagation of the coronary thrombus and reduces re-occlusion risk |
| ***Statin*** | ***High-intensity statin always (‚Č§ 24h)*** [2]. Atorvastatin 80 mg stat | Early pleiotropic effects: plaque stabilisation, ‚Üďinflammation, ‚Üďendothelial dysfunction. Late: lipid-lowering |
| ***ACEI/ARB*** | ***ACEI/ARB always (‚Č§ 24h)*** [2]. Ramipril or perindopril | ‚ÜďVentricular remodelling, ‚Üďafterload, ‚Üďneurohormonal activation. Mortality benefit especially if ‚ÜďLVEF or anterior MI |
| ***MRA*** | ***MRA if LVEF ‚Č§ 40% + HF/DM*** [2]. Eplerenone 25‚Äď50 mg | Blocks aldosterone ‚Üí ‚Üďfibrosis, ‚Üďremodelling. Mortality benefit (EPHESUS trial) |
| **Morphine** | ***IV morphine*** [2] if nitrates insufficient. ***IV Maxolon 5‚Äď10 mg*** antiemetic ¬Ī sedation [2] | ‚ÜďDistress ‚Üí ‚Üďadrenergic drive ‚Üí ‚ÜďSVR, ‚ÜďBP, ‚Üďrisk of ventricular arrhythmia [2]. Use cautiously ‚ÄĒ delays absorption of oral P2Y‚āĀ‚āā inhibitors |

***S/E of LMWH/UFH***: ***heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (HIT), osteoporosis, hyperkalaemia*** [1]

#### B. Reperfusion Therapy in STEMI [1][2]

***"Time is muscle"*** ‚ÄĒ the earlier reperfusion occurs, the more myocardium is salvaged. The two strategies are **primary PCI** (preferred) and **thrombolysis** (if PCI unavailable).

```mermaid
graph TD
    A["STEMI Confirmed"] --> B{"PCI-capable centre within 120 min?"}
    B -->|Yes| C["Primary PCI"]
    C --> C1["Aim door-to-balloon time <= 90 min"]
    B -->|No| D{"Symptom onset < 12h?"}
    D -->|Yes| E["Thrombolysis"]
    E --> E1{"Successful reperfusion?"}
    E1 -->|"Yes: resolution ST >= 50%, pain relief, AIVR"| F1["Routine PCI within 2-24h"]
    E1 -->|"No: ongoing pain, persistent ST elevation"| F2["Rescue PCI stat"]
    D -->|"No: > 12h"| G["Medical management unless ongoing ischaemia"]
    
    A --> H["Special Situations"]
    H --> H1["Cardiogenic shock: PCI irrespective of onset time"]
    H --> H2["Late presenter 12-24h with ongoing ischaemia: consider PCI"]
```

##### Primary PCI [1]

***Indications in STEMI*** [1]:
- ***Primary PCI (aim door-to-balloon time ‚Č§ 90 minutes)***:
  - ***Present < 12 hours after onset of chest pain***
  - ***Clinical and/or ECG evidence of ongoing ischaemia between 12‚Äď24 hours of onset***
  - ***Cardiogenic shock / severe acute HF (irrespective of onset time)*** [1]
- ***Rescue PCI: within 3 hours after failed thrombolysis*** [1]
- ***Post-thrombolytic PCI: within 24 hours after successful thrombolysis to reduce re-infarction rate*** [1]

**PCI Procedure** [1]:
- ***Pre-med: DAPT*** [1]
- ***Vascular access: femoral artery vs radial artery (preferred due to lower bleeding risk: radial artery is paired with ulnar artery, and can be compressed easily against radius)*** [1]
- ***Coronary angiography: inject contrast at mouth of coronary artery*** [1]
- ***PTCA: balloon + stent placement*** [1]

**Stent types** [1]:

| Type | Detail | DAPT Duration |
|---|---|---|
| ***Drug-eluting stent (DES)*** | ***Drugs reduce neointimal proliferation ‚Üí reduce in-stent restenosis. Drugs: paclitaxel (antiproliferative), sirolimus*** [1]. Standard of care | ***12 months ¬Ī extra 18 months if no S/E (late stent thrombosis risk)*** [1] |
| ***Bare metal stent (BMS)*** | ***30% risk of re-stenosis*** [1] | ***4‚Äď6 weeks*** [1] |

***BMS used if: high bleeding risk / cannot take DAPT (e.g. anticipated surgery within 12 months)*** [1]

***Pre-PCI: DAPT (aspirin + ticagrelor) ¬Ī heparin ¬Ī GPIIb/IIIa inhibitors (only in STEMI)*** [1]

***GPIIb/IIIa inhibitor (IV abciximab / eptifibatide): if heavy clot load*** [1]. Mechanism: blocks the final common pathway of platelet aggregation (glycoprotein IIb/IIIa receptor mediates fibrinogen cross-linking between platelets)

***Post-PCI (with stent)*** [1]:
- ***DAPT (aspirin + ticagrelor) for 12 months (DES)***
- ***Lifelong aspirin 80 mg/day*** [1]

**PCI Complications** [1]:
- ***Overall mortality < 0.5%*** [1]
- ***Puncture: pseudoaneurysm, aortic dissection, coronary artery dissection, myocardial infarction***
- ***Balloon: in-stent restenosis (15%) ‚ÄĒ due to elastic recoil and neointimal hyperplasia***
- ***Stenting: stent thrombosis (1‚Äď2%), stent infection (rare)***

##### Thrombolysis [1][2]

***Indications: STEMI with symptom onset within 12h + PCI not available within 2h from diagnosis*** [1]
***NOT used in NSTEMI/UA*** [1] ‚ÄĒ because there is no complete thrombotic occlusion; the thrombus is platelet-rich, non-occlusive, and thrombolysis may paradoxically worsen things

**Choice of agent** [1]:

| Agent | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ***Tenecteplase (TNK-tPA)*** | ***Fibrin-specific*** | Single bolus (weight-adjusted). ***Need LMWH cover*** [1]. Most commonly used |
| ***Alteplase (tPA)*** | ***Fibrin-specific*** | Infusion protocol. ***Need LMWH cover*** [1] |
| ***Reteplase (rPA)*** | ***Fibrin-specific*** | Double bolus protocol. ***Need LMWH cover*** [1] |
| ***Streptokinase*** | ***Fibrin non-specific*** | Cheaper. ***Cannot give with IV heparin (long half-life: combined use = bleeding risk)*** [1]. Cannot be re-used within 6 months (antigenic) |

***Signs of successful reperfusion*** [1][2]:
- ***Clinical: chest pain subsides***
- ***Biochemical: early CPK peak*** [1]
- ***ECG: accelerated nodal/idioventricular rhythm (AIVR), resolution of ST elevation of ‚Č• 50% in the worst ECG lead 90 min post-fibrinolytic*** [1][2]

***S/E: allergy/anaphylaxis (2%), haemorrhagic stroke (1%)*** [1]

***Post-thrombolysis pathway*** [1]:
- ***Successful reperfusion ‚Üí routine PCI in 2‚Äď24h***
- ***Failure ‚Üí rescue PCI stat***

<Callout title="Thrombolysis Contraindications ‚ÄĒ Must Know" type="error">

**Absolute C/I** [2]:
- Previous haemorrhagic stroke at any time
- Other strokes/CVA within 3 months (except acute ischaemic stroke within 4.5h)
- Known malignant intracranial neoplasm
- Known structural cerebrovascular lesion (e.g. AVM)
- Active bleeding or bleeding diathesis (does not include menses)
- Suspected aortic dissection
- Significant closed head/facial trauma within 3 months
- Intracranial/intraspinal surgery within 2 months
- Severe uncontrolled HTN unresponsive to emergency therapy
- For streptokinase: prior treatment within 6 months

**Relative C/I** [2]:
- Severe uncontrolled HTN on presentation (BP > 180/110)
- History of chronic severe poorly controlled HTN
- Prior ischaemic stroke > 3 months or known intracerebral pathology
- Traumatic or prolonged (> 10 min) CPR
- Oral anticoagulant therapy
- Major surgery < 3 weeks
- Non-compressible vascular punctures
- Recent internal bleeding (within 2‚Äď4 weeks)
- Pregnancy
- Active peptic ulcer

</Callout>

#### C. Management of NSTEMI/UA [1][2]

***Thrombolysis is NOT indicated in NSTEMI/UA*** ‚ÄĒ ***no benefit, may even be harmful (not thrombotic occlusion ‚Üí no benefit at all)*** [2].

The key decision is **timing of invasive strategy** based on risk stratification:

```mermaid
graph TD
    A["NSTEMI / UA Confirmed"] --> B["Acute Medical Therapy"]
    B --> B1["DAPT + Heparin + BB + Nitrates + Statin + ACEI/ARB"]
    
    A --> C["Risk Stratification"]
    C --> C1["GRACE score / TIMI score"]
    
    C1 --> D{"Risk Level?"}
    D -->|"Very High Risk"| E1["Immediate invasive strategy: coro within 2h"]
    E1 --> E1a["Ongoing ischaemia, haemodynamic instability, cardiogenic shock, VT/VF, mechanical complications"]
    
    D -->|"High Risk"| E2["Early invasive strategy: coro within 24h"]
    E2 --> E2a["GRACE > 140, troponin rise, dynamic ST/T changes"]
    
    D -->|"Intermediate Risk"| E3["Delayed invasive strategy: coro within 25-72h"]
    
    D -->|"Low Risk"| E4["Ischaemia-guided conservative strategy"]
    E4 --> E4a["Non-invasive stress testing before discharge"]
    E4a --> E4b{"Positive?"}
    E4b -->|Yes| E4c["Proceed to ICA"]
    E4b -->|No| E4d["Discharge with OMT and follow-up"]
```

***High-risk features requiring invasive treatment*** [1]:
- ***Refractory angina***
- ***Cardiogenic shock***
- ***Acute pulmonary oedema***
- ***Ventricular arrhythmia***
- ***ST segment changes ‚Č• 0.1 mV***
- ***New bundle branch block***
- ***Elevated troponin > 0.1 mg/mL***
- ***High risk score (TIMI ‚Č• 3, GRACE > 140)*** [1]

***NSTEMI note***: ***ST elevation (> 1 mm) in aVR suggests left main / severe triple vessel disease ‚Üí directly go for CABG*** [1]

---

#### D. CABG (Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting) [1][15]

***CABG indications for NSTEMI/STEMI*** [1]:
- ***Anatomical considerations (can apply SYNTAX score ‚Č• 23 ‚Üí favours CABG)*** [1]:
  - ***Triple vessel disease (‚Č• 70% stenosis)***
  - ***Proximal LAD disease (‚Č• 70% stenosis)***
  - ***Left main disease (‚Č• 50% stenosis) or left main-equivalent disease (proximal LAD + proximal LCx)*** [1]
- ***Post-MI mechanical complications*** [1]:
  - ***Ventricular septal rupture (VSR)***
  - ***LV free wall rupture / aneurysm***

***Mortality: number of vessels ‚ÄĒ 1VD < 2VD < 3VD < LMS disease*** [2]

| Aspect | CABG | PCI |
|---|---|---|
| **Best for** | 3VD, LMS, proximal LAD, DM, LV dysfunction | 1‚Äď2 VD, non-proximal LAD, surgical high risk |
| **SYNTAX score** | ‚Č• 23 favours CABG | < 23 favours PCI |
| **Graft conduit** | LIMA-to-LAD (gold standard: 90% 10y patency), saphenous vein grafts (60‚Äď70% 10y patency) | Drug-eluting stent |
| **DAPT duration** | Aspirin lifelong, no mandatory P2Y‚āĀ‚āā inhibitor long-term (unless stented) | DAPT for 12 months (DES), then aspirin lifelong |
| **Advantages** | More complete revascularisation, better long-term survival in complex disease | Less invasive, shorter recovery, lower procedural risk |
| **Disadvantages** | Major surgery (sternotomy, CPB), longer recovery, higher procedural risk (stroke 1‚Äď2%) | In-stent restenosis, stent thrombosis, incomplete revascularisation in complex anatomy |

***IHD is a common cause of functional mitral regurgitation*** ‚ÄĒ ***papillary muscle displacement, restricted leaflet closure, annular dilation*** from LV remodelling post-MI [15]. Significant MR may require concurrent mitral valve repair at CABG.

### PART III: LONG-TERM MANAGEMENT POST-ACS

#### A. Secondary Prevention [2]

This is the **lifelong** pharmacological and lifestyle strategy after any ACS event.

##### Long-Term Drug Therapy [2]

| Drug | Regimen | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ***DAPT = Aspirin + P2Y‚āĀ‚āā inhibitor*** | ***Aspirin: administered indefinitely*** [2]. ***P2Y‚āĀ‚āā blocker: administered for ‚Č• 12 mo if any stent used (mandatory), 1‚Äď12 mo even if no PCI done*** [2] | ***Clopidogrel 75 mg QD, prasugrel 10 mg QD, or ticagrelor 90 mg BD*** [2]. ***Caveat: clopidogrel interacts with PPI ‚Üí inhibit CYP2C19/3A4 activation of clopidogrel prodrug ‚Üí treatment failure*** [2] |
| ***ő≤-blockers*** | ***Given to all stable patients if no C/I*** [2]. ***C/I: bradycardia, AVB, ‚ÜďBP, asthma*** [2] | ***NOT C/I in HF, COPD, peripheral vascular disease*** [2] |
| ***ACEI/ARB*** | Given to all post-ACS patients, especially if ‚ÜďLVEF, anterior MI, DM, HTN | ‚ÜďVentricular remodelling, ‚Üďmortality |
| ***High-intensity statin*** | ***Statin always, regardless of serum cholesterol level*** [2] | Target LDL < 1.4 mmol/L (ESC 2019) |
| ***MRA*** | ***If LVEF ‚Č§ 40% + HF/DM*** [2]. Eplerenone | Monitor K‚Āļ and renal function |
| **Anticoagulation** | ***Warfarin if ‚ÜĎembolic risk (e.g. LV thrombus, AF)*** [2]. Consider DOAC for AF | "Triple therapy" (DAPT + OAC) for limited period if concomitant AF, then step down |

##### Risk Factor Modification [2]

- ***Smoking: drastic ‚ÜďMI risk after just 1 year of smoking cessation, doubles 5-year mortality*** [2]
- ***Hyperlipidaemia: high-dose statins for aggressive ‚Üďlipid (regardless of serum cholesterol level) ‚Üí ‚Üďmortality*** [2]
- ***Lifestyle: regular exercise, maintain ideal body weight, Mediterranean diet*** [2]
- ***Other co-morbidities: good control of HTN and DM*** [2]

##### Mobilisation and Rehabilitation [2]

- ***Takes 4‚Äď6 weeks to replace necrotic tissue by fibrotic tissue ‚Üí restrict physical activities until then, offer cardiovascular rehabilitation*** [2]
- ***Usually: mobilise in 2d, discharge in 3‚Äď5d, resume work in 4‚Äď6w*** [2]

##### Post-MI Risk Stratification [2]

- ***LVEF by echo ‚Üí guide further management to ‚ÜďLV remodelling + ‚Üďarrhythmia risk*** [2]
- ***Residual ischaemia ‚Üí stress test: pre-discharge or symptom-limited stress 2‚Äď3w post-MI*** [2]
- ***Arrhythmia during convalescent phase by 24h ECG for VT/frequent ventricular arrhythmia*** [2]

**ICD indications post-MI** [2]:
- LVEF ‚Č§ 30‚Äď35%, ‚Č• 40 days post-MI, on optimal medical therapy, NYHA II-III, good functional status with expected survival > 1 year

<Callout title="High Yield Summary ‚ÄĒ Management of IHD">

1. **Stable IHD**: Prognostic (aspirin + statin ¬Ī ACEI/ARB) + Symptomatic (GTN PRN + BB 1st line ¬Ī CCB ¬Ī 2nd line agents). Revascularise if high-risk anatomy (LMS, 3VD, proximal LAD) or refractory symptoms
2. **ACS Initial Mx (SAQ!!)**: Admit CCU, bed rest, monitoring, rule out DDx. MONA-B + heparin + statin + ACEI/ARB
3. **STEMI reperfusion**: Primary PCI (door-to-balloon ‚Č§ 90 min) preferred. Thrombolysis if PCI unavailable within 120 min and onset < 12h. Rescue PCI if lysis fails. Routine PCI 2‚Äď24h after successful lysis
4. **Thrombolysis NOT for NSTEMI/UA** ‚ÄĒ no complete occlusion, no benefit, may be harmful
5. **NSTEMI/UA**: Risk-stratify (GRACE/TIMI) ‚Üí immediate/early/delayed invasive vs ischaemia-guided conservative
6. **PCI**: DES preferred (DAPT 12 months + lifelong aspirin). BMS only if high bleeding risk / anticipated surgery
7. **CABG**: LMS ‚Č• 50%, 3VD, proximal LAD, SYNTAX ‚Č• 23, DM with multi-vessel disease, mechanical complications
8. **Secondary prevention post-ACS**: DAPT (aspirin + P2Y‚āĀ‚āā) ‚Č• 12 months ‚Üí lifelong aspirin. BB, ACEI/ARB, high-intensity statin for ALL. MRA if LVEF ‚Č§ 40%. ICD if LVEF ‚Č§ 30‚Äď35% ‚Č• 40d post-MI
9. **Nitrate C/I**: HOCM, PDE5 inhibitors. **BB C/I**: acute pulm oedema, 2nd/3rd degree HB, asthma, acute HF decompensation
10. **Clopidogrel + PPI interaction**: PPI inhibits CYP2C19 ‚Üí ‚Üďclopidogrel activation ‚Üí treatment failure. Use pantoprazole (less interaction) or switch to ticagrelor

</Callout>
